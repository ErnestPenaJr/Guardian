Approach C: Page-by-Page Visual Overhaul

Redesign each page individually, starting with highest-traffic flows. Fast visual wins, but risk of drift between early and late pages.

How Approach C Differs
Each page is treated as its own mini-project. You pick the most important page, redesign it completely (new layout, new styling, new interactions), ship it, then move to the next. No shared infrastructure changes upfront. Bootstrap stays until a page is individually converted. Colors, components, and patterns are decided per-page and may diverge.

Approach C Trade-offs

Pros

  • Fastest first impression — Login looks great in Sprint 1
  • Incremental delivery — each sprint ships a polished page
  • Lower risk per sprint — only touching one area at a time
  • Easy to pause — can stop after any sprint with a partially improved app

Cons

  • Jarring transitions — new pages next to old pages feel broken
  • Style drift — decisions made in Sprint 1 may not match Sprint 4
  • Bootstrap persists longer — dual system until every page is converted
  • Cleanup pass needed — inconsistencies accumulate between pages
  • More total work — fixing drift and doing cleanup adds overhead
